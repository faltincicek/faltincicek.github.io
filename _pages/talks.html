--- layout: archive title: "Selected Talks and Presentations" permalink: /talks/ author_profile: true --- Here you can find some of the talks I've given that I consider important.

  1. Atomic Wires on the Hydrogenated Silicon Surface
    February 19, 2025 - Quantum Days 2025, University of Toronto, Toronto, Ontario, Canada
    Presented a poster about electronics structures of dimer wires and their applications in their buckled configurations on the silicon surface.

  2. Silicon Dimers on the Hydrogenated Surface
    April 30, 2024 - Condensed Matter Physics Seminar, University of Alberta, Edmonton, Alberta, Canada
    Gave a talk about electronics structures of dimer wires on the silicon surface.

  3. Atomically Defined Wires on P-Type Silicon
    June 2, 2022 - International Conference on Electron, Ion and Photon Beam Technology and Nanofabrication (EIPBN), New Orleans, Louisiana, USA
    Gave a talk about precise atom-scale patterning and dimer wires made one atom at a time and won the best scanning probe micrograph award.

  4. Atomically Defined Wires on P-Type Silicon
    March 14, 2022 - APS March Meeting, Chicago, Illinois, USA
    Gave a talk on the electronics structures of atomic wires that I made one atoms at a time on the hydrogenated silicon surface.

  5. Silicon Dangling Bond Pairs as Quantum Random Number Generators
    July 1, 2021 - NIST iSPM^3, Virtual
    Gave a talk on how silicon dangling bonds can be used as random number generators (RNGs).

  6. A Study of Silicon Dangling Bond Pairs in Search of a True Random Number Generator
    June 9, 2021 - CAP Congress, Virtual
    Presented a poster about how silicon dangling bonds can be used as random number generators (RNGs) at the virtual congress of Canadian Association of Physcisits .

  7. Synthesis and Characterization of Quasi-Two-Dimensional Chromium Sulfides
    April 29, 2019 - University of North Dakota, Grand Forks, North Dakota, USA
    My masters defense, where I talked about quasi-two-dimensional chromium sulfides with emphasis on their synthesis and characterization.

  8. Working in Two Dimensions - 3 Minute Thesis
    March 15, 2019 - University of North Dakota, Grand Forks, North Dakota, USA
    I attended the 3 minute thesis competetion and explained my masters research about two dimensional materials in 3 minutes without using jargon.

  9. Synthesis and Characterization of quasi 2D-Chromium Sulfide
    March 04, 2019 - APS March Meeting, Boston, Massachusetts, USA
    Presented my masters research at the annual American Physical Society (APS) March Meeting.

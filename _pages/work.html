--- layout: archive title: "Work Experiences" permalink: /work/ author_profile: true --- Here you can find key projects and experiences from my career so far.
  • 2019 - present, Graduate Researcher at the Department of Physics, University of Alberta, Canada
    • Operated and maintained ultra-high vacuum, low-temperature (~4.5 K) scanning tunneling microscopy (UHV LT-STM).
    • Repaired malfunctioning parts such as rough pump, UHV direct-current sample heater and practiced ventilation and baking of the UHV system. Replaced faulty turbo pump, bad capacitors of power supplies etc.
    • Took part in upgrading the LT-STM to QPlus non-contact atomic force microscopy (AFM).
    • Managed lab's cryogenics (liquid nitrogen and helium) consumption and maintained helium recovery plant.
    • Made single atom sharp STM tips (needles) with electrochemical etching and field ion microscope (FIM).
    • Developed LabView programs to ease my job in the lab such as pressure reader, camera view of STM from two sides, and cryostat's level and temperature reader.
    • Passivated silicon surface with monolayer hydrogen and made atomic patterns one atom at a time.
    • Lead a project about making atomic wires on the silicon surface with LT-STM and conducted molecular orbital analysis through scanning tunneling spectroscopy (STS).
    • Explained the agreement of molecular analysis of my measurements with theory in collaboration with theorists.
    • Devloped a numerical simulation of the atomic wires I made and coded it in Python.
    • Analyzed atomic wires data with Fourier transform to study their electronic bands and effective masses.
    • Made atomic switches that function as a one-electron process with applications to atomic memory elements and random number generation.
    • Analyzed the atomic switcher's transition rates statistically to determine their randomness level.
    • Characterized surface quality of nanolithographed samples prepared in the clean room with photolithography and e-beam lithography with STM.
    • Learned density functional theory (DFT) and applied to what I would be normally doing in the lab during the Covid pandemic lock-down.
    • Worked with Slurm Workload Manager (sbatch) on supercomputers of Digital Research Alliance of Canada (previously ComputeCanada) to perform DFT calculations.
    • Attended several international conferences to present my research.
  • 2017 - 2019, Graduate Researcher at the Department of Physics and Astrophysics, University of North Dakota, USA
    • Assembled a home-built chemical vapor deposition (CVD) system with spare rough and turbo pump, argon gas cylinder, and tube furnace for quartz tubes.
    • Synthesized quasi-two-dimensional chromium sulfides with the CVD system I built.
    • Characterized two dimensional molecules I deposited on mica with X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS), X-ray powder diffraction (XRD), and atomic force microscopy (AFM).
    • Collaborated with groups from the Petroleum Engineering and Chemistry departments in two separate projects where I analyzed their samples with XPS, which yielded two papers.
  • 2014 - 2017, Undergraduate Researcher at the Department of Physics, Bilkent University, Turkey
    • Studied the Gross-Pitaevski-Poisson (GPP) system to model compact astrophysical objects as Bose-Einstein Condensates (BCE) with computational methods.
    • Numerically simulated a solvable model of Maxwell's Demon and conducted statisctical analysis.
  • 2015 Summer, Summer Intern at the Department of Physics, Middle East Technical University, Turkey
    • Studied NASA's data on neutron stars and pulsars with FTOOLS software on linux OS.
